About

What is Girls Who Lead?

A Pitt student organization founded Spring 2026, affiliated with the national nonprofit Her Rising Initiative.

Girls Who Lead @ Pitt (GWL) exists to close the opportunity gap for women in leadership. We bring together students who are interested in policy, law, business, technology, and advocacy, and we give them the spaces, skills, and connections to actually do something with those interests.

We are a campus chapter of Her Rising Initiative, a national nonprofit that supports women's leadership development at colleges and universities across the country. That means our programming, values, and community are part of something bigger than a single campus.

GWL is in its founding year, which means there is real room to shape what this organization becomes. Members who join early help define the culture, the events, and the direction.

Our
Committees.

Two member-led committees launch once GWL reaches 20 active members. Here is what they do and how to get involved.

GWL has two standing committees established by our constitution: Programming and Outreach & Membership. Committees are the primary way general members take on leadership responsibilities. Each committee is 3 to 8 members with one officer liaison and a chairperson selected through an open application process. Committees launch by midterms of the fall semester in which membership reaches 20 active members.
📋
Programming Committee
Plans and executes GWL's events, workshops, and speaker series across the academic year.
The Programming Committee is responsible for the events that make GWL worth showing up for. Working alongside the VP and President, this committee turns the chapter calendar into a reality: speaker panels, skill workshops, networking nights, and civic co-hosted events. Members learn event planning, speaker outreach, and logistics coordination in a real-stakes setting.
What committee members do
Research and recommend event ideas each semester
Assist with speaker outreach and scheduling
Handle event logistics: booking spaces, AV, and supplies
Set up and run events on the day of
Write post-event recaps for the chapter record
🌸
Outreach & Membership Committee
Grows the chapter, welcomes new members, and keeps the GWL community connected beyond meetings.
The Outreach & Membership Committee handles everything that happens before and after events: recruiting new members, running tabling and campus presence, supporting onboarding, and keeping the member community engaged between meetings. This committee is a great fit for people who enjoy relationship-building, social media, and making people feel like they belong.
What committee members do
Table at campus fairs and departmental events
Manage GroupMe and social media member communications
Run the new member onboarding process each semester
Track member attendance and good-standing status
Assist with partnerships and community organization outreach

How to join a committee

Committee membership is open to all general members in good standing. Here is how the process works.

📌
Eligibility: You must be a general member in good standing to apply for a committee seat. Good standing means attending at least 50% of general meetings in the current semester. Committees are not available until GWL reaches 20 active members, which we expect by midterms of Fall 2026.
Step 01
Application opens
When membership hits 20, the officer team announces committee applications. The call goes out via GroupMe and email, with a one-week window to apply.
Step 02
Submit your interest
Fill out a short form indicating which committee you are interested in and why. No experience required. We are looking for genuine interest and willingness to show up.
Step 03
Chairperson application
If you want to lead a committee, submit a separate chairperson application. The officer team reviews all chair applicants and votes on the final selection.
Step 04
Placement announced
Placements are announced within one week of the application deadline. Each committee holds 3 to 8 members. If both committees fill, a waitlist is maintained.
Step 05
First committee meeting
Committees hold their kickoff meeting within two weeks of formation. The officer liaison for each committee is present to set direction and answer questions.
Timeline: Committees are expected to be fully formed by midterms of the fall semester once the 20-member threshold is crossed. If you join GWL early and stay in good standing, you will be first in line when applications open.
